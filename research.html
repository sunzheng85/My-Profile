  • Current Research

  • Our group has recently made efforts into creating channels for propagation of polariton condensates. We have successfully designed these channels in shape of long narrow wires, square pillars and rings.

  • Movie showing the flow of polariton condensate in the ring channel. Complex dynamics emerge as pendulum like oscillations near the bottom of the ring at early times which gives way to rotational current and a four-fold symmetric mode around the ring at later times. (Click on the movie to enlarge)

  • We are investigating the effect on polariton condensates in long wires when a current is injected or a bias voltage is applied across these wires.

  • We are part of an interdisciplinary multi-university collaboration which aims to establish room temperature polaritons in two dimensional atomic crystals as a bridge from the discovery of new many-body phenomena to novel photonic technologies -- the polaritronics.

  • In the past, we have demonstrated long distance (~ 1 mm) flow of polaritons in a potential gradient. This is possible because of the long lifetime (~ 200 ps) of the polaritons in our samples.

  • We have shown half-quantum circulation in a spinor polariton condensate trapped in a two-dimensional trap. Interference fringes showing coherence across the entire ring condensate. The fringes arise because two copies of the image from the two legs of a Michelson interferometer are overlapped.
